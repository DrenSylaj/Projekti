PRISHTINA.

Prishtina

Pristina is the capital and largest city of Kosovo with a population of more than 200.000 people. The city is located on the western part of Balkans and close to many Balkan countries such as Serbia, North Macedonia, and Montenegro. Pristina’s history dates back to prehistoric times and used to be the most significant city in Balkans during Rome rule. The city grew to be a popular tourism destination with its spectacular historical landmarks and cultural heritage.

Places to Visit

Some of the most frequented and unique places to be in Prishtina

Cathedral

The Cathedral of Saint Mother Teresa in Pristina is a Roman Catholic cathedral constructed in Pristina, Kosovo in 2007.

National Library

The building of the National Library of Kosovo was inaugurated on 25 November 1982. Designed by Andrija Mutnjaković.

Kurrizi

Kurrizi or "The Spine", is a massive high-rise apartment building followed by chains of shops, stacked on top of each other

Pallati Rinis

Formerly named Boro and Ramiz, is a multi-purpose hall. It includes two indoor arenas.

Germia Park

Germia is a regional park located in the northeast of Pristina, Kosovo, and covers an area of 62 square kilometres.

Newborn

(NEWBORN) is a typographic sculpture, it was unveiled on 17 February 2008, the day that Kosovo declared its independence.
